Objectives

This is an advanced undergraduate course on modern foundations of analysis. The official course syllabus asks that we cover the general theory of integration, based on the concept of measure. We will begin with an introduction to general topology, focusing on Euclidean space and spaces of functions. We will work toward integration over manifolds, in particular, the important generalized Stokes' theorem and its applications.

Prerequisites

101a, 102a, 203a (or 101b, 102b, 213).

Textbooks

We will be working from Andrew Browder's Mathematical analysis : an introduction. Hard copies have been available for in-library 2-hour loan since the end of October (sorry for the delay).

A useful reference in Chinese is 《实变函数论》， 周民强编著.

For further reading and other perspectives, Measure and integral by Wheeden and Zygmund is recommended.
