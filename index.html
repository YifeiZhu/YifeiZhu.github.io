Personal information

My research interests are in algebraic topology and related fields, particularly in its connections to algebraic geometry and number theory via objects such as formal groups, elliptic curves, and modular forms.

I received my Ph.D. from the University of Minnesota in 2013. My advisor was Tyler Lawson.

From 2013 to 2016, I was a visiting assistant professor at Northwestern University.

Research

Morava E-homology of Bousfield-Kuhn functors on odd-dimensional spheres, in progress

We give explicit presentations for the completed E-homology of the Bousfield-Kuhn functor on odd-dimensional spheres at chromatic level 2, and compare them to the level 1 case.

Deformation structures and norm coherence, in preparation

We generalize Ando's construction of H∞ complex orientations on Morava E-theories associated to Honda formal group laws over Fp. We show the existence of such an orientation on any Morava E-theory associated to a formal group law over an algebraic extension of Fp.
